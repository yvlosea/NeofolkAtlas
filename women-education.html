Women's Education Initiative

Kaithi script preservation, Magahi documentation, writing fellowships, and skill development for women.

Program Status: All women's education initiatives are in planning phase and will launch upon securing dedicated funding. No programs are currently running.
Six Programs

Education that Honors Women's Knowledge

Grant-Eligible
📜

Kaithi Preservation

The ancient script of Bihar, used by women for centuries to keep household records. We are documenting, teaching, and digitizing Kaithi manuscripts.

  • Script workshops for women
  • Manuscript digitization
  • Research fellowships
  • Publication of findings
12-week intensive
Grant-Eligible
🗣️

Magahi Documentation

Magahi, the language of the region, carries oral histories, folk songs, and women's narratives. Systematic documentation and language preservation.

  • Oral history collection
  • Folk song archiving
  • Dictionary compilation
  • Community storytelling
Ongoing project
✍️

Writing Fellowships

Residential writing programs for rural women—fiction, non-fiction, memoir, and journalism. Supporting women to tell their own stories.

  • 2-week residencies
  • Mentorship from writers
  • Publication support
  • Writing circles
Quarterly cohorts
👩‍🍳

Culinary Training

Nutrition education, food entrepreneurship, and preservation of traditional Bihari cuisine. Connecting food systems to economic opportunity.

  • Nutrition science
  • Food business skills
  • Traditional recipe preservation
  • Market linkages
8-week program
💹

Financial Literacy

Banking basics, savings, investment, and entrepreneurship. Empowering women to manage finances and start small businesses.

  • Banking & savings
  • Micro-enterprise basics
  • Bookkeeping skills
  • Loan navigation
6-week course
💻

Digital Literacy

Smartphones, computers, and internet for market access, communication, and online learning. Bridging the digital divide.

  • Basic computer skills
  • Smartphone navigation
  • Online market access
  • Digital safety
4-week course

Why This is Unique

Very few institutions are systematically working on Kaithi script preservation or Magahi language documentation. This makes Panchmahala's Women's Education Initiative genuinely distinctive—and highly grantable.

  • First systematic Kaithi preservation program in Bihar
  • Community-led Magahi documentation
  • Women as knowledge keepers and creators
  • Integration of traditional wisdom with modern skills
  • Publication and research outputs
"Grant gold for foundations focused on women's empowerment, language preservation, and cultural heritage."
