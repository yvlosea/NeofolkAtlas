Strategic Positioning

Why Panchmahala?

Location choice is strategic. This area can become an education hub.

Proximity to Patna

90km from state capital. Accessible for partnerships, talent recruitment, and government liaison.

Rail Access via Mokama

Mokama Junction on Delhi-Howrah main line. Direct connectivity to major metros.

Ganges Proximity

Cultural significance, water access, spiritual anchor for holistic education.

Family-Held Land

18 Katha family land available at sustainable cost. No speculative pressure.

Township Expansion

Bihar government planning infrastructure expansion. Early-mover advantage.

Education Vacuum

No quality residential educational institutions in 50km radius. Underserved community.

Concept Inspiration & Goal

Solarpunk, hope, and a future built with nature

Our idea is based on the solarpunk eco-futurism idea, where technology works with nature not against it. The solarpunk revolution is a need for the Indian growing economy to provide self-sustenance, walkable architecture and community travel support. Our goal starts with the school, but it doesn't stop until India is a nation that is again renowned for its sustainability, comfort and quality education. We look to the sun and ask for a future where to the youth, the stars are also visible.

Inspiration reference: "Dear Alice" by THE LINE on YouTube.

Hidden Letter

A long-term note from the founder

Change has always begun as something unrealistic.

Every major transformation in history was first dismissed as idealistic, impractical, or impossible, until someone chose to build it anyway.

At iNHET, we are working toward a future where education is accessible, dignified, and deeply connected to culture, sustainability, and technological progress.

We envision a solarpunk future for India, one where students live in walkable towns, receive affordable and equal education, engage with meaningful work, preserve cultural heritage, and help build cleaner, smarter communities.

This project was born from devotion to our country and frustration with watching talent leave, communities decline, and education become increasingly inaccessible.

We believe India can become a global leader in education, innovation, peace, and cultural preservation, not by copying existing systems, but by building better ones.

While this website may showcase conceptual renders and AI-assisted visualizations, we strongly oppose long-term dependence on generative AI in creative industries. These visuals exist only to communicate ideas in their earliest stages and will be replaced with human-led design work as development progresses.

Everything presented here is goal-oriented and built with execution in mind.

We have an active team working across multiple initiatives, and we welcome conversations with educators, architects, policymakers, investors, researchers, and anyone who wants to understand these projects more deeply.

If you believe education can be redesigned for the better, we invite you to build with us.

Founder, iNHET
Yashveer Vats Gaurav
