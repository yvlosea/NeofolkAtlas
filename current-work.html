Panchmahala Learning Tower

India has no shortage of classrooms. What it lacks is an education system that treats students as complete human beings: curious, physical, creative, and capable of building the communities they come from.

Featured Design

The Beno Tower

A hexagonal learning tower that serves as the visual identity and flagship hub of the Panchmahala campus. Designed with climate-responsive features including passive cooling, natural ventilation, and a coloured pergola roof.

Campus Vision

Interactive Masterplan

Explore the phased development of the Panchmahala campus on 18 Katha of land in Mokama, Bihar.

Phase 1 — Legally Secured (2026-2028)
Phase 2 — Aspirational Expansion (2028-2031)

This is currently empty, undeveloped land. No structures exist on site as of 2026. All layouts shown are planning concepts subject to change.

Beno Tower
01
Hexagonal learning tower — flagship vertical hub and visual identity of campus
Key Functions
  • Ground Floor: Library, reading spaces, archives
  • First Floor: Research rooms, writing spaces
  • Second Floor: Multi-purpose learning rooms
  • Roof Pavilion: Open-air learning, events, stargazing
  • Design: hexagonal form, passive cooling
Academic Block
02
Structured education centre — operational complement to the Tower
Key Functions
  • Classrooms for NIOS curriculum
  • Robotics and AI labs
  • Language learning spaces
  • Computer labs and exam spaces
  • Skill development workshops
Hostels
03
Affordable residential accommodation for students and visitors
Key Functions
  • Shared student rooms
  • Visiting faculty accommodation
  • Common lounges and study rooms
  • Laundry and recreation areas
  • Terrace spaces for community
Garden Corridor
04
Green campus connector and outdoor learning environment
Key Functions
  • Walking paths and outdoor study areas
  • Rest areas and meditation zones
  • Shade structures and micro farming
  • Herbology and Ayurvedic plants
  • Visual breathing space for campus
Primary Path
05
Main pedestrian and mobility spine connecting the campus
Key Functions
  • Walkable pedestrian spine
  • Future electric shuttle movement
  • Bicycle circulation paths
  • Accessibility-first design
