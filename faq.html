Questions & Answers

Clear answers to common questions about Panchmahala, our trust, and how to get involved.

General Questions

Panchmahala is not a traditional school. It is a hybrid educational ecosystem combining NIOS-accredited formal academics with residential skill programs, cultural preservation, and community engagement. Students live on campus, combining structured academics with practical learning in agriculture, craft, technology, and civic participation.

Yes. Panchmahala is being developed by the Indian Neofolk Humanitarian Education Trust (iNHET), a registered non-profit organization. We are currently in the process of obtaining 12A and 80G certifications to make donations tax-deductible for Indian donors.

Panchmahala is located in the village of Panchmahala, near Mokama, in Patna district, Bihar, India. It is approximately 90 kilometers east of Patna city and accessible via the Mokama Junction railway station on the Delhi-Howrah main line. The campus is being built on 18 Katha (approximately 32,000 square feet) of family-held land.

Admission & Students

Student applications will open in 2026 when our pilot phase launches. The admission process will involve: (1) NIOS registration for formal academics, (2) iNHET application including a personal statement and interview, (3) Family consultation to ensure alignment with our residential model. We prioritize students from rural Bihar who demonstrate curiosity, commitment to community, and willingness to engage in all aspects of campus life.

Yes, residential living is central to our model. Phase 1 (2026-2028) will include basic hostel accommodation for approximately 20 students. Phase 3 (5-10 years) envisions expanded residential facilities for 200+ students. Hostels will be designed for shared living, with community kitchens, common study areas, and integration with the natural surroundings.

Initially, we will focus on secondary school students (ages 14-18) pursuing NIOS certifications. Over time, we plan to expand to include: (1) Bridge programs for younger students, (2) Post-secondary vocational training, (3) Adult education and women's programs, (4) Community workshops open to all ages.

Donation & Support

Yes, we welcome donations from individuals, foundations, and corporations. We are currently in the process of obtaining 12A registration and 80G tax exemption status in India. In the meantime, we can accept donations through bank transfer. For larger donations or CSR partnerships, please contact us directly at inhetedu@zohomail.in to discuss recognition and reporting requirements.

Donations are allocated across four priority areas: (1) Construction and infrastructure (50%), (2) Educational programs and faculty (25%), (3) Student scholarships and operational costs (15%), (4) Reserve and sustainability fund (10%). We are committed to financial transparency and will publish annual reports detailing all expenditures.

Site visits are welcome for serious donors, potential partners, and educators. Please contact us at least two weeks in advance to schedule a visit. We can arrange transportation from Mokama Junction or Patna. During your visit, you will meet the team, see the construction progress, and understand the local context firsthand.

Construction & Timeline

Construction is happening in three phases. Phase 1 (2026-2028) includes the Library Tower, basic hostel, and kitchen—enough to launch our pilot with 20 students. Phase 2 (2028-2031) adds expanded classrooms, the amphitheatre, and women's education facilities. Phase 3 (2031-2036) completes the student town vision with capacity for 200+ residents.

Yes. The 18 Katha (approximately 32,000 square feet) of land is family-held and committed to the trust for educational purposes. The land is free from encumbrances and located in a rural setting ideal for our campus vision. We are in the process of formalizing the long-term lease arrangement with the trust.

The Library Tower and initial structures are being designed by a team of architects committed to vernacular, climate-responsive design. Construction is being managed through a combination of professional contractors for structural work and local masons and craftspeople for finishing. We prioritize local employment and training, making the construction process itself an educational opportunity.
